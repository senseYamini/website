Less is More: Improving WiFi Localization Accuracy with Fewer Scans

COMSNETS 2026 (Accepted for presentation)

Traditional WiFi-based indoor localization pipelines rely on dense and frequent scanning to achieve good accuracy. However, aggressive scanning drains device battery, increases channel contention, and scales poorly as the number of users grows. In this work, we show that more scans do not necessarily mean better localization. Through extensive experiments across diverse indoor environments, we uncover a surprising insight: only a small subset of carefully selected scans captures most of the localization signal strength. We systematically analyze the relationship between scan count, spatial diversity, and localization error. We propose a lightweight scan-scheduling strategy that adapts scan frequency to environmental dynamics and user motion, achieving high accuracy with minimal overhead.
